    How to Use This Calculator

    This scientific calculator supports both basic arithmetic and advanced mathematical functions. Type directly using your keyboard or tap the buttons on screen. Press Enter or = to compute, and Escape to clear.

    The calculator automatically closes unclosed parentheses when you press equals — so sin(45 will correctly compute as sin(45°). Use the ANS button to reuse the last result in a new calculation, and click any entry in the History panel to load it back.

    Switch between DEG (degrees) and RAD (radians) using the mode button in the top-left of the display. The ⚙ settings panel lets you choose font size and how many decimal places to show.

    📐

    Trigonometry — sin, cos, tan

    Trigonometric functions relate the angles of a triangle to the ratios of its sides. They are fundamental in physics, engineering, architecture, and many other fields. On this calculator you can use sin, cos, tan and their inverses asin, acos, atan.

    By default the calculator works in degrees. Switch to radians for calculus and physics work — in radians a full circle is 2π ≈ 6.283. A quick reference of common angles:

    Logarithms & Exponentials

    A logarithm answers: to what power must a base be raised to get a given number? log computes base-10, so log(100) = 2 because 10² = 100. ln computes the natural logarithm (base e ≈ 2.718), used in calculus, biology and finance.

    Logarithms and exponentials are inverse operations: 10^log(x) = x and e^ln(x) = x. Essential for solving equations where the unknown is an exponent — compound interest, population growth, radioactive decay, earthquake magnitude.

    Use xʸ to raise any number to a power, and √ for square roots. Fractional exponents also work: 27^(1/3) gives the cube root of 27 = 3.

    Tips & Tricks

    Implicit Multiplication

    Omit × before parentheses or constants. 2π, 3(4+1) and 2sin(30) all work.

    Auto-close Parentheses

    Forgot a closing parenthesis? Pressing = auto-closes them. sin(45 → sin(45).

    Chain with ANS

    Press ANS to insert the last result into a new expression for multi-step calculations.

    Keyboard Shortcuts

    Enter=calculate, Backspace=delete, Escape=clear. All digits and operators work from keyboard.

    Decimal Precision

    Open ⚙ Settings to fix decimal places (0–15). Great for money calculations needing 2 decimals.

    History

    Last 50 calculations saved automatically. Click any entry to load it back into the calculator.
